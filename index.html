How Quadratic Funding Works

Quadratic funding is a mathematically optimal mechanism for funding public goods in a democratic way. It works by calculating a project's matching funds based on the square of the sum of the square roots of contributions.

This means that projects with more diverse support (many small contributors) receive more matching funds than projects with the same total amount from fewer contributors.

The formula: Matching Funds = (Sum of √contributions)²

This creates incentives for projects to build broad community support rather than relying on a few large donors.
